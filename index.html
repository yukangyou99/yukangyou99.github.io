Biography

I'm Kangyou Yu (Calvin), a second-year CS Master in FourEyesLab (directed by Prof. Tobias Höllerer) at University of California, Santa Barbara (UCSB). Prior to joining UCSB, I received my BSc degree in Information and Computing Science from Xi’an Jiaotong-Liverpool University (Suzhou, China) and University of Liverpool (Liverpool, United Kingdom) in July 2022 and worked as an undergraduate research assistant at X-CHI Lab, supervised by Prof. Hai-Ning Liang and Dr. Wenge Xu .

Last summer, I worked as a research intern in the APEX Group, supervised by Prof. Mingming Fan (The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou), China). I also worked as a remote research intern in the VVISE Lab, supervised by Prof. Wolfgang Stuerzlinger (Simon Fraser University, Canada) and Prof. Anil Ufuk Batmaz (now at Concordia University, Canada).

My research interest lies on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and AR/VR/MR and focus on making intelligent system/devices understand human behaviors/capabilities, creating interactive system which can be adaptive to different interaction environments and contexts, and providing more feedback (Haptics, Auditory...) to design more natural interactive techniques for AR/VR. I am also interested in games technologies, visualization and exergames.
